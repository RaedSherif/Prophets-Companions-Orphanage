Prophet's Companions Orphanage's Staff

Prophet's Companions Orphanage is home to a dedicated team of professionals who are passionate about giving every child in our care a brighter future. Our team is committed to providing a loving, safe, and nurturing environment, empowering each child to reach their full potential. With a deep understanding of the cultural and social fabric of Egypt, our staff works tirelessly to create a supportive and compassionate community.

Get to know our staff:
Amina Hassan – Director With over 15 years of experience in social work and child welfare, Amina has dedicated her life to helping vulnerable children. She holds a master’s degree in Social Work from Cairo University and has led numerous community projects supporting underprivileged youth. Amina’s vision is to create a space where every child feels valued, safe, and hopeful for the future.
Karim El-Sayed – Educational Coordinator Karim is a former schoolteacher with a background in primary education. He believes that education is a cornerstone for every child’s growth and future success. Having grown up in Alexandria, Karim understands the unique challenges facing young Egyptians today. At Hope for Tomorrow, he works to create engaging, culturally relevant educational programs that help children build confidence and a love for learning.
Fatima Ahmed – Lead Caregiver Known for her warmth and patience, Fatima has over 12 years of experience working with children in various caregiving roles. She brings expertise in early childhood development and has trained in trauma-informed care to help children overcome difficult pasts. Fatima's nurturing presence makes her a beloved figure in the orphanage, where she ensures each child feels seen and heard.
Mariam Abdel-Rahman – Health and Wellness Coordinator Mariam is a registered nurse and a licensed nutritionist with a passion for child healthcare. After studying nursing at Ain Shams University, Mariam worked in pediatric care, focusing on malnutrition and children’s mental health. At the orphanage, she coordinates medical checkups, wellness programs, and nutritional plans to keep every child healthy and strong.
Youssef Moustafa – Community Outreach Manager Youssef leads our community outreach initiatives, helping to raise awareness and build partnerships with local businesses, volunteers, and donors. With a degree in Public Relations and years of experience in nonprofit work, Youssef is the bridge between the orphanage and the broader community. His goal is to foster a network of support around each child and bring in resources that benefit the orphanage.
Layla Fathy – Cultural and Recreational Activities Coordinator Layla organizes all cultural and recreational activities for the children, from arts and crafts to field trips that explore Egypt’s rich history. A graduate of the Faculty of Arts at Helwan University, Layla believes in the power of creativity and culture to inspire hope and build resilience. She ensures that every child has opportunities to express themselves, learn about their heritage, and build joyful memories.
